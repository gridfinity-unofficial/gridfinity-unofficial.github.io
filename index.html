Get started with online generators!

  • Perplexing Labs' Gridfinity Generator
    Generate custom bins, baseplates, lids, and more - just input your dimensions!
  • Design bins and baseplates with gridfinitygenerator.com
  • GridfinityCreator lets you generate STL or STEP files for several types of customizable Gridfinity components.

You're a developer? Get started with some code!

  • Build123d
    • PaulBone/gfthings - Generate several different kinds of gridfinity compatible objects for 3D printing
    • Ruudjhuu/gridfinity_build123d - This repository contains python modules to create gridfinity capable objects
  • CadQuery
    • kmeisthax/gridfinity-cadquery - This is a port of Zach Freedman's Gridfinity storage system to CADQuery
    • michaelgale/cq-gridfinity - Python classes to create gridfinity compatible parameterized components such as baseplates and boxes
  • OpenSCAD
    • vector76/gridfinity_openscad - Recreation of Zack Freedman's gridfinity system in OpenSCAD
    • ostat/gridfinity_extended_openscad - Accurately reproduce the original Gridfinity bins while adding extra features and customization options
    • kennetek/gridfinity-rebuilt-openscad - A ground-up port (with a few extra features) of the stock gridfinity bins in OpenSCAD.

The best things in life are integer multiples of 42x42x7mm.

Gridfinity could be your workshop's ultimate modular storage system to keep you productive, organized, and safe. It is free, open source, and almost 100% 3D printable.

Origins of Gridfinity

Alexandre Chappel's Assortment System, licensed under CC-A-NC-SA, partly inspired Zack Freedman's initial designs of Gridfinity. The Gridfinity designs were first released in the video "Gridfinity: Your Ultimate Modular Workshop is FREE!" as a framework for the community to extend, released under the MIT license.

Now Gridfinity is in the hands of a thriving community that continually adapts it to their needs. We invite you to join this community by using and adapting the system!
